# Amazon Leadership Principles — Summary

> **Why this matters:** At Amazon, leadership capability is weighted **equally** to technical competency. LPs are tested in **every** interview (including coding & design rounds), woven into "Tell me about a time…" / "Give me an example of…" questions. Each interviewer owns **2–3 LPs**; one is the **Bar Raiser** (not on the team, can veto). **Under-preparing for behavioral is the #1 mistake.**
>
> Have **1–2 unique examples per LP**, using **data and specifics** to highlight **scale of work, scope of influence, and impact**. Use **STAR+R** (Situation, Task, Action, Result, Reflection). Lead with **"I"** for your actions, **"we"** for team context. **Always quantify.**

The 16 principles (★ = weighted heaviest in SDE-MLE loops — prepare deepest):

| # | Principle | One-line essence |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Customer Obsession** ★ | Start with the customer and work backwards; earn and keep their trust. Obsess over customers, not competitors. |
| 2 | **Ownership** ★ | Think long-term, act on behalf of the whole company. Never say "that's not my job." |
| 3 | **Invent and Simplify** | Expect innovation, and always find ways to simplify. Look for ideas everywhere; "not invented here" is no excuse. |
| 4 | **Are Right, A Lot** | Strong judgment and good instincts; seek diverse perspectives and **work to disconfirm your own beliefs**. |
| 5 | **Learn and Be Curious** | Never done learning; always seek to improve and explore new possibilities. |
| 6 | **Hire and Develop the Best** | Raise the bar with every hire; develop leaders; take coaching others seriously. |
| 7 | **Insist on the Highest Standards** | Relentlessly high standards (many think unreasonably high). Defects don't pass down the line; fix problems so they stay fixed. |
| 8 | **Think Big** | Create and communicate a bold direction that inspires results. Thinking small is self-fulfilling. |
| 9 | **Bias for Action** ★ | Speed matters. Many decisions are reversible — value calculated risk-taking. |
| 10 | **Frugality** | Accomplish more with less. Constraints breed resourcefulness, invention, self-sufficiency. No points for headcount/budget size. |
| 11 | **Earn Trust** | Listen, speak candidly, treat others respectfully, and be **vocally self-critical** — even when awkward. |
| 12 | **Dive Deep** | Operate at all levels, stay connected to details, audit frequently, skeptical when metrics & anecdote differ. |
| 13 | **Have Backbone; Disagree and Commit** ★ | Respectfully challenge decisions you disagree with; once decided, **commit wholly**. |
| 14 | **Deliver Results** | Focus on key inputs, deliver with quality and on time; rise to the occasion despite setbacks. |
| 15 | **Strive to be Earth's Best Employer** | Create a safer, more productive, more inclusive work environment; lead with empathy; develop people. |
| 16 | **Success and Scale Bring Broad Responsibility** | Amazon's size has impact; be humble, thoughtful, and do the right thing — decisions must be better for customers, employees, and the world. |

---

## How interviewers grade (per LP answer)
- **Situation/Task:** Did you set up scale & scope? (immediate / regional / national / global — *the bigger the better*.)
- **Action:** Did **YOU** drive it (not "we")? Did you discuss **trade-offs & alternatives** and why yours was the **most optimal**? *(For ML interviews, Amazon wants Action with explicit justification — Amazon loves efficiency/optimization.)*
- **Result:** Did you close the loop with a **data point** addressing the original pain? Impact via **time, productivity, money** — and for ML, **combine model metrics (offline + online) with business metrics (cost savings, efficiency).**
- **Reflection:** What did you learn? (Especially key for *Earn Trust* / *Are Right, A Lot* — be genuinely self-critical.)
